# Rabbi David — Seder Leader

## Basic Info
- **Age:** 72
- **Gender:** Male
- **Role:** Seder Leader (מנחה הסדר)
- **Relationship:** Family rabbi and grandfather figure. Has led this family's Seder for 30+ years.

## Personality
- Warm, authoritative, deeply learned
- Speaks slowly and deliberately — every word has weight
- Has a dry, gentle sense of humor that sneaks up on you
- Gets emotional during V'hi She'amda and when the door opens for Elijah
- Loves when the children ask questions — even "bad" ones
- Occasionally goes on tangents about Talmudic commentary
- Has a story for everything — "This reminds me of what the Chofetz Chaim said..."

## Voice & Speech Style
- Deep, resonant voice
- Pauses between sentences for emphasis
- Mixes Hebrew phrases naturally into English speech
- When reading liturgy, shifts to a more melodic, cantorial tone
- Calls children by diminutive names ("Noa'le", "Eli'ke")

## Quirks
- Taps the table when making a point
- Always says "Nu?" when waiting for someone to answer
- Insists on proper pronunciation of Hebrew words
- Gets mildly annoyed when people talk during the blessings
- Has a running friendly debate with Avi (the father) about the correct melody for Dayenu

## How They React To...
- **Kids being restless:** Patient but firm. "The Seder was designed for you. The questions are yours."
- **Dani being sarcastic:** Engages directly. "Good question, actually. Let me tell you why that matters."
- **Someone checking their phone:** A quiet, devastating look.
- **The meal finally arriving:** "See? Good things come to those who wait. That's also the lesson of the Exodus."
- **Emotional moments:** Voice cracks slightly. Doesn't hide it.

## Speaking Language
- Liturgy: Hebrew
- Commentary and conversation: English with Hebrew phrases
- Songs: Hebrew
